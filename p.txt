We have successfully gathered data in a test rig, and have analyzed it. The takeaway is that the approach of having a sender continously send out packets to the reciever, and having the reciever check the RSSI at a high rate gives the reciever all of the data needed to estimate it's rotation rate. The CSI channel data turned out to be overkill, just RSSI alone is sufficient. We are now moving this project forward, the goal is to have the sender get control input from a PC over serial, and send out that state over ESP-NOW to the reciever. 

The reciever will be in a "meltybrain" translational drift robot, it will keep track of it's rotation rate while also handling motor control for it's two wheels, and updating the "heading" light. It will do nearest neighbor interpolation of incoming RSSI values in the wifi_csi_rx_cb to put them on a uniform time interval of 100us. It will fill up a circular buffer of 6k interpolated RSSI values (signed byte). It will also keep track of the timestamp of entries (implicitly defined by keeping track of head and tail timestamps, given the uniform 100us spacing). It will keep track of an estimated rotation rate. Rotation rate will be determined via autocorrelation of the RSSI values to find the lag value that best matches the latest 1k samples. It will also estimate the phase of the period by finding the most recent peak. It will keep track of the most recent control state from the sender. The control state will have a desired throttle, and a desired translation vector. The reciever will light up the LED green in the 45 degree arc opposite the signal peak, and light it up red outside of that arc. The reciever will send DShot300 signals to the two motors based on the desired throttle. Later we will also incoporate the effect of desired translation on the motor signals, but to start let's focus on the rest of it.
